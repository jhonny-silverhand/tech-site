## Settings won't fix fundamentals, but bad settings will hold you back

No configuration file makes up for game sense or crosshair placement — but the wrong settings can genuinely cap how well your mechanics translate into hits. These are starting points to tune from, not universal answers.

## Video settings for the best FPS-to-visual-quality tradeoff

Valorant is CPU-bound more than most shooters, so most graphics sliders matter less here than in a typical AAA game. Prioritize in this order:

1. **Material Quality: Low** — biggest FPS gain for the least visual cost
2. **Texture Quality: Medium** — keep this reasonably high, it barely costs FPS and helps you read enemy models
3. **Detail Quality: Low**
4. **UI Bloom: Off** — reduces visual noise around bright elements, which helps target acquisition
5. **VSync: Off** — adds input lag for essentially no benefit if you're already running above your monitor's refresh rate

## Sensitivity: find your number once, then stop touching it

Constantly adjusting sensitivity resets the muscle memory you're trying to build. A common starting method:

- Set **eDPI (in-game sensitivity × mouse DPI) between 200–320** as a reasonable competitive starting range
- Spend at least a week at one setting in the practice range before deciding whether to change it
- If you're consistently overshooting flicks, lower it slightly; if you're under-turning on wide swings, raise it slightly — small changes, not big jumps

## Crosshair: function over appearance

A cluttered crosshair with excessive outline, bloom animation on fire, or a large center dot gets in the way of precise placement. A simple starting point: small center dot, thin short lines, no outline, firing error and movement error visualization turned on so you can see when your crosshair is telling you accuracy is degraded.

## Mouse settings outside the game matter too

- Turn **Windows "Enhance pointer precision" off** — it adds acceleration that fights consistent aim
- Match your **mouse polling rate to your monitor's refresh rate** where your mouse allows it, to avoid unnecessary input smoothing

## The setting that helps more than people expect

**Minimap size and rotation** — a minimap set to rotate with your view is easier to read at a glance mid-fight than a fixed north-up minimap, since it matches what you're already looking at instead of requiring a mental rotation.
